## CRITICAL Issues (Fix Immediately)

### 1. Homepage Hero Headline Is Generic — No Unique Differentiator

**Page:** Homepage (above the fold)

**Before:**
> Fresh Seasonal Fruits, Delivered to Your Door by Tomorrow

**After:**
> Singapore's #1 Farm-Fresh Fruit Delivery — At Your Door by Tomorrow Morning

**Why this works:**
- Adds geographic authority claim ("#1" in Singapore) which builds instant credibility
- "Farm-Fresh" is more vivid and specific than "Seasonal"
- "Tomorrow Morning" is more specific than "Tomorrow" — specificity converts
- Positions against competitors immediately

---

### 2. Homepage Sub-headline Tries to Do Too Much

**Page:** Homepage (below hero headline)

**Before:**
> Hand-picked daily from trusted farms since 1982. Free delivery over $60. First order? Use FRUITS10 for 10% off.

**After:**
> Hand-picked daily from trusted farms since 1982.
> Free delivery on orders over $60 • New here? Get 10% off with code FRUITS10

**Why this works:**
- Breaking into two visual lines improves scannability
- The bullet separator (•) makes the two offers visually distinct
- "New here?" is more conversational and welcoming than "First order?"
- Current version crams 3 different messages (heritage, delivery, discount) into one run-on sentence

---

### 3. Only ONE CTA Across the Entire Homepage: "Shop Fresh Fruits"

**Page:** Homepage

**Before:**
> "Shop Fresh Fruits" (used everywhere — hero, footer, category sections)

**After — use varied CTAs by context:**
- Hero: **"Browse Today's Fresh Picks →"** (curiosity-driven, implies daily rotation)
- Omakase section: **"Surprise Me — Choose a Box"** (playful, matches the omakase concept)
- Best Sellers section: **"See What's Popular This Week"** (social proof CTA)
- Footer: **"Start Your First Order — 10% Off"** (incentive CTA)

**Why this works:**
- One repeated CTA creates "banner blindness" — visitors stop seeing it
- Context-specific CTAs match the visitor's mindset at each scroll position
- Variety increases the chance of one CTA resonating with the visitor's motivation
- Current conversion benchmark: specific CTAs convert +161% better than generic ones

---

### 4. Flash Deal Countdown Timer Shows 00:00:00:00

**Page:** Homepage

**Issue:** The "big day flash deals" section shows a countdown timer at `00 Days 00 Hrs 00 Min 00 Sec` — meaning the deal has expired or was never configured. This actively **damages credibility**. An expired countdown timer signals either a fake urgency tactic or a neglected website.

**Fix:**
- Either remove the countdown entirely, OR
- Set it to a real upcoming date and tie it to an actual promotion
- If keeping it, add copy explaining what happens when the deal ends ("Next flash deal drops Friday")

### 5. About Page Has No Story — Just Features

**Page:** About

**Before:**
> "We believe in bringing the freshest, highest quality fruits directly from farms to your doorstep. Every fruit is hand-selected to ensure our customers receive only the best nature has to offer."

**After:**
> "In 1982, our founder started with a single fruit cart at Pandan Loop. Four decades and 3 million fruits later, we're still doing what we love — hand-picking the best seasonal fruits and getting them to Singapore families by tomorrow morning. No warehouses. No middlemen. Just farm-fresh fruit, delivered with care."

**Why this works:**
- Tells a specific origin story (Pandan Loop, 1982) which builds emotional connection
- Uses concrete proof points (3 million fruits, four decades) instead of vague claims
- "No warehouses. No middlemen." creates a clear differentiation point
- The current version reads like it was written by any fruit delivery company in the world — zero personality

## HIGH PRIORITY Issues

### 6. Social Proof Is Weak and Unverifiable

**Page:** Homepage

**Issues with current testimonials:**
- Only 4 reviews displayed, one of which is from "Lex Na" (the owner/team member — this damages credibility)
- Average rating shown as 4.3/5 but no source cited (Google? Internal?)
- "Be the first to leave a review" appears on ALL Omakase products — signals zero social proof for the core product
- No review count shown (e.g., "Based on 247 reviews")

**Fix:**
- Remove the owner's review immediately
- Add review count: "4.8 stars from 320+ reviews on Google"
- Link to Google Reviews for verification
- Add at least 6-8 genuine reviews with full names, neighborhoods, and specific details
- Remove "Be the first to leave a review" — either hide the review section on products with no reviews, or seed with real customer reviews

### 7. About Page — Media Section Is in Chinese Without Context

**Page:** About

**Issue:** The media/news section shows Chinese-language headlines (水果商聘疫情失业者, 椰子热销 生意翻倍, etc.) without any English translation or context. For an English-language website, this creates a jarring disconnect.

### 8. Omakase Fruit Boxes Category Page Has ZERO Copy

**Page:** Omakase Fruit Boxes

**Issue:** The entire category page is just 3 product cards with names and prices. No description of what "Omakase" means, no explanation of the concept, no reason to choose one size over another.

**Add:**
- A category introduction: what Omakase means, how the boxes work, what to expect
- Differentiate the three sizes beyond just price (e.g., "4-5 fruits" vs "7-8 fruits" vs "10-12 fruits")
- Add a "What's in this week's box?" teaser or past box gallery
- Include a "Perfect for" line (gifting, personal treat, office snacks)

**Suggested category intro:**
> **What is an Omakase Fruit Box?**
> "Omakase" means "I'll leave it to you" in Japanese. Each week, Boss Kelvin hand-selects the freshest premium fruits available — so every box is a surprise. Think Korean strawberries one week, Japanese muscat grapes the next. Choose your size, and we'll do the rest.

### 9. Value Props Section (Homepage) Is Generic

**Page:** Homepage

**Before:**
> - Trusted Sourcing: Reputable suppliers to ensure consistent quality and freshness.
> - Wide Selection: A broad range of seasonal and everyday fruits in one place.
> - Customer-First: Friendly support and careful packing with every order.

**After:**
> - **Sourced Fresh Daily:** Every fruit hand-picked from farms we've trusted for 40+ years. If it's not fresh, we don't ship it.
> - **100+ Varieties, Updated Weekly:** From everyday favourites to rare Japanese imports — one shop for everything.
> - **Packed with Care, Delivered with a Smile:** Bruise-free packaging, next-day delivery, and a real human on WhatsApp if anything's off.

**Why this works:**
- Adds specificity (40+ years, 100+ varieties, next-day, bruise-free)
- Includes proof/mechanism (hand-picked, WhatsApp support)
- Creates personality ("If it's not fresh, we don't ship it")
- The originals could describe literally any online fruit store

## LOW PRIORITY Issues

### 15. Product Names Need Descriptive Subtitles
"Omakase Fruit Box – Small / Medium / Large" tells customers nothing about what they get. Add weight ranges or fruit counts (e.g., "Small — 4-5 premium fruits, serves 1-2").

### 16. Blog Articles Need Conversion-Focused Titles
Current: "How to Pick the Perfect Watermelon: 7 Expert Tips" — fine for SEO but could be punchier for on-page engagement. Consider adding a benefit hook.

### 17. Missing Urgency/Scarcity Elements
No "limited stock" or "seasonal availability" messaging anywhere. For a fruit business where stock is genuinely limited and seasonal, this is a missed opportunity for authentic urgency.

### 18. No Delivery Information Page Link in Hero Area
Free delivery above $60 is mentioned in the sub-headline but there's no easy way to learn more about delivery zones, timing, or cutoff times from the hero area.
